SKILLS & APPROACH

Product Methodology:

  • Understand job to be done → identify shortest path with minimal friction → map across business → tie to strategy → identify 1-3 opposing metrics (make trade-offs explicit) → make it visual and shareable
  • Remove process steps before optimising
  • Question constraints from first principles ("no sacred cows")
  • Use prototypes to demonstrate potential before asking for investment
  • Build system that works now to lock in gains, with evolution plan

Corporate Navigation:

  • Don't ask executives to greenlight projects. Rather show signal that they'd need to stop you from continuing. Keep costs low to avoid asking for budget and fly under the radar. Changes risk from 'approving unproven project' to 'killing promising project.'
  • Find decision-makers, understand their agenda, align with it

Prioritisation:

  • Focus on outcomes and constraints rather than frameworks
  • I don't like spreadsheet prioritisation as it provides fake certainty. You need clear, simple answers to trade-offs within constraints to achieve outcomes.
